Design

Design a creative personalized piece of furniture. Every person is unique and by right their style of decorating should be personalized. As long as something works I am open to helping someone create their own ideas. I do not believe the rules of traditional decorating need apply to everyone. If you have an antique window you want to make into a wall hanging or a coffee table, let's do it. Did you find some awesome iron works at a yard sale and want it on your wall, why not? Or is it as simple as taking a vintage dresser handed down through the years and giving it a face lift? Paint and new hardware go a long way towards making a unified and coheisive design that is unique to you.

Repurpose

I touched on repurpose in *Design* and I must reiterate that I am open to taking something from its traditional definition of use and making it into something useable and beautiful. I have made plaques from antique door knockers, tufted benches from out dated oak coffee tables, key hooks from dovetailed drawers, and dinning tables from bed rails. I am a child of the ideal "waste not" why should a completely good piece of wood be feul for the fire when it can spend another lifetime with a different use. Furniture is expensive and the vintage forms of furniture making and cabinet making left treasure troves of useable and lasting materials for generations.

Repair

Repairing vitage furniture provides with chic and stylish furniture and decor at a fraction of the cost. Two of the most common forms of repair I am confronted with are upholstery and caning. Caning is a form of weaving wooden strips to create a decorative and comfortable piece of furniture. I am hopefully in a position to learn this ancient craft from the geriatric experts as long as I do not compete with them. I personally outsource this type of repair locally to a non-profit who funds our senior citizen center. I personally reupholster smaller furniture, chairs and smaller sofas or settees.
